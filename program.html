Program

A registration for the weekend includes both Thursday and Friday evening Critics’ Choice Grand Tastings, all the Friday and Saturday daytime sit-down conducted tastings, seminars, and luncheons and the Saturday evening black-tie Grand Award Banquet.

This year’s event will include dozens of great wines in fun and educational conducted tastings. Each wine is presented by the owner and/or winemaker. All are included in your registration fee.

• Italy’s antinori: A comparative tasting of the 2007, 2004, and 1997 vintages from two of Italy’s original “super Tuscans:” Tignanello and Solaia.Owner Piero Antinori will be on stage.

• France’s Guigal: Philippe Guigal will present six wines from the prestigious cellars of E. Guigal to show the variety and depth of his family’s Rhône Valley vineyards.

• California’s to kalon: A tasting of four Cabernet Sauvignons, all sourced from Napa’s famed Beckstoffer To Kalon Vineyard, including Carter Cellars, Paul Hobbs Winery, Schrader Cellars and Tor Kenward Family Wines.

• France's Burgundy: An exciting tasting of four white wines from Burgundy to show style and depth: Bruno Colin Chassagne-Montrachet La Maltroie 2011, Louis Jadot Meursault Charmes 2011, Olivier Leflaive Puligny-Montrachet Les Folatières 2010, William Fèvre Chablis Bougros 2012. Owners will be on stage to talk about these sensational wines.

• Wine Spectator’s 2013 top 10 wines:
Selected by Wine Spectator’s board of editors and
published in the Dec. 31, Top 100 issue.
An annual highlight.

• Chile’s Icons: A tasting of classic wines from four of Chile’s top wineries: Casa Lapostolle, Concha y Toro, Viña Almaviva and Montes. From Cabernet Sauvignon to Carmenere to Syrah, these wines represent the best of Chile.

• Italy’s Masi: A vertical tasting of four great vintages of Amarone from Masi, presented by Raffaele Boscaini. An extraordinary treat.

• Douro Destiny: Matt Kramer celebrates one of the greatest transformations in modern wine history by presenting three dry table wines from Portugal's Douro Valley. Once consecrated only to Port wine, the vast Douro Valley today is reaching ever-greater heights creating remarkable table wines, both red and white.

• Food and Wine Pairing: With celebrity chefs, including José Andrés, Emeril Lagasse, Eric Ripert, and restaurateur Danny Meyer. A fun event for all.

• Wine Stars: Throughout the weekend there will be individual presentations of great wines by renowned vintners from around the world, each a star in the world of wine. Famous names include Catena Zapata's Nicolás Catena, Bruno Prats of Prats & Symington, California Zinfandel master Larry Turley and Marcassin's Helen Turley and John Wetlaufer.

Important Notice: The New York Wine Experience is sponsored by the Wine Spectator Scholarship Foundation, which underwrites grants and scholarships to students pursuing careers in the wine industry. Attendance is tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.
