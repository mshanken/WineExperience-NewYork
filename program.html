Program

A registration for the weekend includes both Thursday and Friday evening Critics’ Choice Grand Tastings, all the Friday and Saturday daytime sit-down conducted tastings, seminars, and luncheons and the Saturday evening black-tie Grand Award Banquet.

This year's event will include dozens of great wines in fun and educational conducted tastings. Each wine is presented by the owner and/or winemaker. All are included in your registration fee.

Italy’s antinori

A comparative tasting of the 2007, 2004, and 1997 vintages from two of Italy's original "super Tuscans": Tignanello and Solaia. Owner Piero Antinori will be on stage.

FRANCE'S GUIGAL

Philippe Guigal will present six wines from the prestigious cellars of E. Guigal to show the variety and depth of his family's Rhône Valley vineyards.

Matching Masters.
A food-and-wine pairing with our experts—chefs José Andrés, Emeril Lagasse and Eric Ripert and restaurateur Danny Meyer—as they compete to earn the audience's vote for their culinary creativity.

Douro Destiny.
 Matt Kramer celebrates one of the greatest transformations in modern wine history by presenting three dry table wines from Portugal's Douro Valley. Once consecrated only to Port wine, the vast Douro Valley today is reaching ever-greater heights, creating remarkable table wines both red and white.

  • Wine & Soul Douro White Guru 2012
  • Lemos & Van Zeller Douro Quinta Vale D. Maria 2011
  • Quinta do Vale Meão Douro 2011

The Grand Award Banquet.
The crowning event of the weekend, is a lavish black-tie evening with special wines, great food and star
entertainment. The banquet is thenatural conclusion to a weekend filled with new wine experiences and
new friends. Wine Spectator will recognize the restaurateurs who have earned the Grand Award in 2014,
which honors the world's greatest restaurant wine lists.

Entertainment.
CHICAGO...one of the most successful bands in America. Get ready to sing with and dance to their mega hits such as "If You Leave Me Now," "You're The Inspiration," "Does Anyone Really Know What Time It Is," "Saturday In the Park," and so many more. This is a big show with a big sound. An exciting ending to a perfect weekend.

Important Notice: The New York Wine Experience is sponsored by the Wine Spectator Scholarship Foundation, which underwrites grants and scholarships to students pursuing careers in the wine industry. Attendance is tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.
